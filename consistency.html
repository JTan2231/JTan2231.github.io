Consistency

  • An absolute consistency all throughout a system is what makes it perform optimally
    • Interests you have that are consistent throughout life are reflective of the manner in which you should be consistent
      • Reflective of the invariant of your character/personality, i.e. "who you are"
      • This is what people (should) be referring to when they say to "be yourself"
    • This is often attempted at scale with companies -- an example of consistency within organization
      • Incessant meetings, "alignment", "in sync", etc.
      • To make social consistency happen in an organization is the purpose of a leader -- "This is how all of you should behave, and I am why."
        • This can't be comprehensive, think it needs a separate essay
  • Something from Christopher Alexander's Timeless Way of Building
    • The whole book, really
  • Atomic Habits
  • If you're doing it right, it shouldn't be difficult
    • Goes back to an absolute/thorough consistency
    • We're pattern recognizers
      • If something doesn't fit the form (i.e. if something is inconsistent with the rest of the process) then it will stick out/not feel right
    • "Catching the vibe" as a means of understanding process
      • Learning how to do this is probably the best way of approaching uncertainty (i.e. life)
  • How to build this sort of consistency?
    • Not sure this is a solved problem
    • A willingness to touch the flame -- getting burned
      • "Just do things"
      • Darwin's quote on the most successful animals being those most receptive to change
      • Examples of bravery/aggression/etc. being successful survival tactics
    • Adapting to the problems you currently face
      • The future is too uncertain to account for things we haven't seen yet
      • Using the past as an indicator of what might happen in the future
    • Your behavior as a function of your environment -- your health is an infrastructure problem
      • Your environment is the physical/social stimulus you routinely experience
      • Experimenting with different eating/sleeping/activity habits to find the best fit -- again, trial + error
      • Choosing a tribe of people with whom to associate -- this could prob be a citation to a different essay
    • Doing what feels right as a metric for measuring how consistent you are
      • If your habits/system/process is good, then it will feel good to follow your impulse -- things will just function "better"
  • Can probably write about how this extends to tools
    • Interactions will only be as deep as the platform allows
    • Same thing as environment?
  • Probably something here that can be linked to trust
    • "Can I trust you to maintain and contribute to the consistency I've set forth?"
